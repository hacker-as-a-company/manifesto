# Hacker as a Company - Manifesto Repository  
**Version:** 1.0 • **Status:** Pre-operational • **Last updated:** 2025-11-15

This repository contains the foundational documents of **Hacker as a Company (HaaC)**: a framework for ethical, autonomous, and legally compliant hackers who operate with the freedom of independents and the clarity of professionals.

HaaC was created to define a new model for hackers:  
**Ethics first. Freedom second. Impact always.**

This repo is the reference point for the philosophy, governance, and community guidelines of the movement.

---

# Purpose of This Repository

The goal of this repository is to gather all core documents that explain:

- The **Hacker as a Company philosophy**
- The **ethical foundations** of the movement
- The **community guidelines** and cultural principles
- The **governance model** during the pre-operational phase
- The long-term vision for autonomous, ethical hackers  
- Standards for transparency, collaboration, and contribution

This is the **source of truth** for the HaaC framework and for all local or global forks.

# What Is Hacker as a Company?

**Hacker as a Company (HaaC)** is a new model for cybersecurity:  
A structured way for hackers to operate **legally**, **ethically**, **freely**, and **professionally**, without sacrificing autonomy or creativity.

A hacker is not a 9-to-5 function.  
A hacker is an autonomous thinker.  
But companies need structure, clarity, and responsibility.

HaaC builds the bridge between:
- **freedom and structure**
- **curiosity and responsibility**
- **creativity and legality**

The movement was born in **Morocco**, but designed to be **global**.

---

# Core Principles

### **Ethics First**  
Trust, legality, consent, and responsibility before everything.

### **Freedom With Structure**  
Autonomy, curiosity, and creativity - protected by ethical boundaries.

### **Transparency**  
Open code, open governance, open discussions whenever possible.

### **Collaboration Over Competition**  
No egos. No ranks. Only collective growth.

### **Impact Over Noise**  
Less bureaucracy, more deliverables.  
Less talking, more building.

See `COMMUNITY_GUIDELINES.md` for details.

# How to Contribute

HaaC is an open-source movement.  
Anyone may propose ideas, open issues, improve documents, or suggest changes.

Ways to contribute:

- Improve or translate documentation  
- Propose enhancements to the framework  
- Create local chapters  
- Suggest new governance models  
- Participate in discussions  
- Help build the platform  

Before contributing, please read:

- `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`  
- `COMMUNITY_GUIDELINES.md`  

Contributions must align with the **ethics-first** foundation.
