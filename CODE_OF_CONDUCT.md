# Hacker as a Company - Code of Conduct
Version 1.0 , 2025  
License: CC BY-SA 4.0

This Code of Conduct defines how members of the **Hacker as a Company (HaaC)** ecosystem
, hackers, entities, contributors, partners, and communities ,
are expected to behave.

It applies in all HaaC spaces:

- online (GitHub, Discord, forums, chats),
- offline (events, meetups, workshops),
- and during all professional engagements carried under the HaaC name or spirit.

This Code of Conduct is a practical extension of the **HaaC Manifesto**:
> Ethics first , freedom protected by structure , impact guided by responsibility.

---

## 1. Core Values

All participants in the HaaC ecosystem agree to uphold these values:

- **Ethics first**  
  No illegal activity, no unauthorized testing, no abuse of access.

- **Respect and inclusion**  
  Treat every person with dignity, regardless of origin, gender, background, or skill level.

- **Responsibility and accountability**  
  Own your actions, your words, and their consequences.

- **Transparency and honesty**  
  Communicate clearly, document your work, and act in good faith.

- **Curiosity, not harm**  
  Explore to understand and protect , not to exploit or damage.

---

## 2. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- all members of the **HaaC Community**,
- all **HaaC Entities** (Hacker as a Company structures),
- organizers and participants of HaaC events,
- contributors in HaaC-related repositories, spaces, and channels,
- any use of the **HaaC brand, name, or frameworks**.

It applies:

- during professional missions,
- during community collaboration,
- and in any context where you represent or invoke HaaC.

---

## 3. Expected Behavior

All participants are expected to:

### 3.1 Respect Others

- Use respectful and inclusive language.  
- Listen actively, do not talk down to others.  
- Welcome differing viewpoints; disagree without attacking people.  
- Respect cultural and personal differences.

### 3.2 Act Ethically and Legally

- Never perform hacking or security testing without **clear, documented authorization**.  
- Respect all scopes, rules of engagement, and legal boundaries.  
- Report vulnerabilities responsibly, through agreed channels.  
- Protect confidential information and personal data.

### 3.3 Communicate Transparently

- Be honest about your skills, limits, and availability.  
- Clarify expectations with clients, partners, and teammates.  
- Document your work, decisions, and results.  
- Admit mistakes quickly and work to correct them.

### 3.4 Support the Community

- Help others learn when you can.  
- Share knowledge in a way that does not endanger users or systems.  
- Encourage ethical behavior and call out harmful practices.

---

## 4. Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are **strictly prohibited** in all HaaC contexts:

### 4.1 Personal Misconduct

- Harassment, bullying, or intimidation of any kind.  
- Discrimination based on gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, religion, or any other protected characteristic.  
- Insults, threats, or personal attacks.  
- Unwanted sexual attention or advances.

### 4.2 Professional and Ethical Violations

- Any form of **illegal hacking**, including:  
  - accessing systems without explicit authorization,  
  - exceeding agreed scope,  
  - retaining unauthorized access.  

- **Privacy violations**, including:  
  - accessing or exfiltrating personal data without strict necessity and consent,  
  - publishing or leaking sensitive information.  

- **Misuse of vulnerabilities**, including:  
  - exploitation for personal gain beyond agreed terms,  
  - selling or trading vulnerabilities found under HaaC engagements to third parties.  

- **Deception**, including:  
  - misrepresenting your identity or qualifications,  
  - falsifying results, reports, or metrics.

### 4.3 Abuse of Power or Position

- Using HaaC status, brand, or access to:  
  - coerce others,  
  - bypass rules,  
  - pressure clients or partners into unethical decisions.

---

## 5. Responsibilities of HaaC Entities

HaaC Entities (“Hackers as a Company”) have additional obligations:

- Ensure that all work is covered by **contracts, clear scopes, and NDAs** where applicable.  
- Keep proper **documentation, logs, and reports** of their activities.  
- Respect **local laws, tax obligations**, and professional regulations.  
- Train and supervise any collaborators so they follow this Code of Conduct.  
- Refuse client requests that would violate ethics, law, or the HaaC Manifesto.

---

## 6. Responsibilities of Clients and Partner Organizations

Clients and partner organizations interacting with HaaC agree to:

- Provide **clear, written scopes** and rules of engagement.  
- Not request or encourage illegal, unethical, or covert activities.  
- Treat HaaC members and Entities with professionalism and respect.  
- Pay fairly and on time for agreed work.  
- Act on serious vulnerabilities and findings in good faith.

---

## 7. Reporting a Problem

If you experience or witness a violation of this Code of Conduct, you are encouraged to:

1. **Document what happened**  
   - Date, time, place (or channel), people involved, what was said or done.

2. **Report through an official channel**, for example:  
   - a designated HaaC community email or form,  
   - a trusted Core Council member or Code of Conduct team.

3. If you are unsure, you may start with:  
   - asking for advice from a trusted community member, without exposing sensitive details.

Reports should be handled **confidentially** and **respectfully**.

---

## 8. Enforcement

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in actions including, but not limited to:

- A private warning.  
- A request for a public or private apology.  
- Temporary or permanent removal from HaaC community spaces.  
- Removal of roles, statuses, or badges.  
- Suspension or termination of participation in HaaC Entities or projects.  
- Notification of employers, partners, or relevant authorities when required by law.

Enforcement decisions:

- should be made by a **designated group** (e.g. Core Council / CoC team),  
- should aim to be **fair, proportionate, and transparent**,  
- may take into account the **intent, impact, and history** of the person involved.

---

## 9. Appeals

Anyone subject to an enforcement action may request a review, if:

- they believe the process was unfair or incomplete,  
- they can provide additional context or information.

A separate person or group (if available) should review the case where possible.
